Solitude, Utah

Solitude, while not as famous as either Alta or Deer Valley, remains one of Utah's hidden gems. It is smaller, but that adds to its charm. In recent years Solitude has turned into an actual "resort", with many lodging options right on the slopes.

One of the charms of Solitude is the backcountry-style area called Honeycomb Canyon. On a snow day you will be able to find many untracked areas to play in. It takes some time to get from the bottom of Honeycomb Canyon back up to the top, but that gives you the opportunity to explore the entire resort while you return for seconds.

The drive to Solitude is one of the most breathtaking (and most harrowing if the weather is bad) you will find anywhere. Making your way fourteen miles up Big Cottonwood Canyon, through a narrow, winding road and up the famous S curve you will eventually arrive at the lower lot of Solitude.

The price of a day will run anywhere between $135 and $160, a significant increase from the $32 that I paid many years ago. It is, however, significantly more affordable than some of the other options in the area.
